Who We Are

About the AI Advisory Committee

A student-led group at Marin Academy dedicated to honest, grounded exploration of artificial intelligence — its opportunities, its risks, and its real-world implications.

Our Purpose

Students Thinking Critically About AI

The AI Student Advisory Committee is made up of students in grades 9–12 at Marin Academy who are passionate about understanding how artificial intelligence shapes our world — not just the technical side, but the human, ethical, and social dimensions.

We believe the most important conversations about AI aren't happening in boardrooms — they're happening right here, among young people who will inherit the world these technologies are building.

Our work focuses on moving past the hype and headlines to engage with AI's real implications: for our school, our community, and the world.

🔍

Critical Inquiry

We ask hard questions about AI rather than accepting it at face value — exploring what it can and can't do, and who it serves.

🌐

Real-World Connections

We bring in practitioners and researchers who work with AI daily — not to lecture, but to have honest conversations.

🤝

Community Engagement

Our events are open to all students and faculty at Marin Academy — no background in tech required.

📚

Informed Perspective

We want MA students to enter college and careers with nuanced, grounded views on AI's role in society.

Our Work

What the Committee Does

From organizing speaker events to advising on AI policy at MA, we work to make sure student voices are part of the conversation.

🎤

Speaker Series

We organize a curated series of talks each semester, bringing in researchers, engineers, founders, and MA alumni to speak candidly about their work in AI — during lunch, open to all.

💬

Honest Conversations

We don't host panels designed to hype AI or bash it. We aim for candid, informed discussions that help students develop their own views — based on real information.

🔗

Alumni Network

We've built relationships with MA alumni now working at Google DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, NVIDIA, and beyond — and we invite them back to share what they've learned.

📢

Community Outreach

We announce events school-wide, coordinate with faculty, and work to make sure all students — regardless of technical background — feel welcome and included.

🏛️

Advisory Role

As an advisory committee, we aim to represent the student perspective in conversations about how AI is — and should be — used in our school community and educational environment.

🌍

Planetary Justice Alliance

Several of our events are co-organized with the Planetary Justice Alliance at MA, connecting AI's impacts to broader questions of environmental and global justice.

Why It Matters

The Questions We're Exploring

Our speaker series isn't just about AI — it's about the world AI is building, and our role in shaping it.

🌏

What does it cost to build AI?

The minerals in AI chips come from somewhere. The power that runs data centers has to come from somewhere. What are the real environmental and human costs behind AI infrastructure?

⚖️

Who does AI serve — and who does it harm?

AI systems make decisions that affect people's lives. Who designs them? Who benefits? And what happens when they go wrong in communities without power to push back?

🎓

What does a career in AI actually look like?

Not the stock-option dream — the real day-to-day. What skills matter? What's exciting? What's frustrating? What should students be learning right now?

💡

Can AI be a force for good?

From detecting dyslexia in K–12 students to expanding financial access in emerging markets — what does responsible, human-centered AI actually look like in practice?
