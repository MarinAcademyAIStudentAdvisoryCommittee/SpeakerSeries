Conflict Minerals & AI Hardware

A Marin Academy alumna now pursuing doctoral research at Oxford and Stanford, Bella studies the intersection of conflict mineral supply chains and AI hardware infrastructure. Her work examines the community impacts of resource extraction on communities affected by the production of AI's physical components — an often invisible dimension of how AI is built.

Featured Speaker

Spotlight: Ava Iannuccillo '20

Ava Iannuccillo

Associate Product Manager · UCSF Dyslexia Center
B.S./M.S. Human Factors Engineering, Tufts University · MA Class of 2020

Ava is a product engineer specializing in human-centered design, scientific research, and emerging technology. She has worked across consumer, medical, and assistive technology sectors, and currently leads product development at UCSF's Dyslexia Center — a statewide digital screening, intervention, and research platform used in K–12 classrooms across California.

The platform translates neuroscience research into practical tools for identifying and supporting students with reading challenges. Ava uses AI in design processes and collaborates with researchers to develop improved machine learning models for children's speech recognition.
